The file aggregated.ods contains the average ratings of 800 fictional characters made by 1,665,664 users of the on-line test at https://openpsychometrics.org/tests/characters/ for 268 different "traits" defined by a bipolar adjective pair. The .ods file can be opened with Open Office Calc, as well as other spreadsheet programs.

Users took the test with the motivation to obtain their best matching fictional character. At the end of the test, but before the user viewed their results, they were asked if they would be willing to take a short survey to help make the quiz better. People who said yes were then asked to select from a list of fictional universes ones they knew. One of the universes they selected (if any) was then assigned to them and they were given characters from that universe to rate 30 times. The image below shows how the page where they made the ratings looked.

The rating was made by moving the slider scale to any value 1 - 100. Where 1 = slider at furthest left position to 100 = slider at furthest right position (50 = centered starting position).

Each user was given 30 character trait-combos to rate, randomly paired.

The data file contains 3 pages. On all pages, rows represent the values for a character, with its ID starting the row. The columns are the average value for that character in the aggregated ratings. The first page is the mean value of the ratings, the second page in the number of ratings for that particular character-trait pairing, and the third page is the standard deviation of the ratings.
